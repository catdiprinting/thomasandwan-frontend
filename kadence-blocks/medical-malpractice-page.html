The Alarming Statistics

Experts estimate that as many as 98,000 people die in any given year from medical errors that occur in hospitals. That's more than die from motor vehicle accidents, breast cancer or AIDS—three causes that receive far more public attention.

Indeed, more people die annually from medication errors alone than from workplace injuries. And, although errors may be more easily detected in hospitals, the problems extend to every health care setting, including day-surgery and outpatient clinics, retail pharmacies, nursing homes and home care.

The Institute of Medicine's study estimated preventable medical errors are the SIXTH LEADING CAUSE OF DEATH in America.
