Compassionate Texas Medical Malpractice Lawyers

We Care, and Our Dedication Shines Through

Representing our clients in the pursuit of justice is both an honor and a privilege. At Thomas & Wan, we define justice as holding grossly negligent hospitals, clinics, doctors, and nurses accountable for their actions in cases of medical malpractice. Our commitment is to ensure that our clients not only receive fair compensation for their losses through legal action but also that these grossly negligent entities reform their policies, training, and practices to prevent others from enduring the same suffering, injury, or loss of life due to malpractice.

Empowering Citizens through the Legal System

We firmly believe that juries play a pivotal role in safeguarding the rights of citizens. In the courtroom, an injured individual stands on equal ground with even the largest hospital corporations. Whether your case can be resolved amicably without the need for a lawsuit or requires a tenacious battle that extends all the way to trial, we're here to stand by your side. Every client, without exception, deserves the highest standard of legal representation and an equitable opportunity to present their case in court.

Linda Laurent Thomas

Since 1987, Linda Laurent Thomas has pursued aggressive legal representation on behalf of injury victims in cases involving personal injuries and wrongful death, medical malpractice, automobile accidents and transportation injuries, work related injuries, environmental and pollution injuries, and defective products. Whether the wrongdoer is a Fortune 500 corporate giant, or a reckless and negligent driver, Thomas has dedicated her career to fighting for individuals to obtain the maximum amount of damages available under the law.

Every case that the firm takes on is handled with the highest level of care and attention. This representation involves all of the phases of a complex personal injury action: the early and thorough factual investigation of the case; the retention of the appropriate expert witnesses; a thorough case value assessment; aggressive pre-trial discovery; alternative dispute resolution; proactive negotiations; jury and bench trials; and appeals. She believes the right to a jury is of utmost importance to preserving the rights of individuals in this country.

Linda Laurent Thomas is a cum laude graduate of South Texas College of Law in Houston and The University of Texas at Austin.
